The question

If measurement is already noisy, does a finer reporting language really matter for coordination? The paper shows the answer is yes.

2

The mechanism

Coarse reporting clusters posterior mass onto a few categories. That raises the probability of exact ties, and ties widen the set of self-consistent cutoffs.

3

The implication

Resolution and accuracy are distinct design margins. Accuracy changes learning, but resolution directly targets selection risk created by discretization.

Benchmark Logic

The paper in one mechanism

The additive benchmark isolates the source of multiplicity cleanly. It is not continuous noise by itself. It is the way the reporting grid creates exact ties in posterior-relevant signals.

Why discretization changes the game

  • Agents first observe a latent noisy measurement of the fundamental.
  • A reporting rule snaps that measurement onto a finite grid of categories.
  • Two independent agents can now land in the same category with positive probability.
  • Near the action boundary, those exact matches flatten the best-response comparison.
  • Flattened boundary incentives allow a whole block of nearby cutoff rules to be self-consistent.

Closed-form benchmark result

Beyond The Benchmark

What remains true outside the benchmark

  • No exact ties, no connected interval multiplicity. Without ties, adjacent cutoff values cannot both be sustained.
  • Ties are the local thickening force. They create slack between adjacent boundary conditions.
  • Tilt is a location and branching force. It comes from directional asymmetry in boundary beliefs and can move or split the equilibrium set.
  • Local flatness recovers the benchmark logic. When the prior is locally diffuse on the relevant scale, tilt becomes small and ties dominate again.

Why It Matters

Four short policy takeaways

The paper is not only about equilibrium theory. It also shows how reporting design can be used as a practical instrument once equilibrium selection matters.

1

Refining reports targets selection risk directly

Holding a cutoff fixed, resolution changes the reporting language rather than the latent measurement. It therefore acts directly on the tie channel.

2

Accuracy upgrades can cut both ways

Better measurement improves learning, but on a fixed coarse grid it can also pile probability mass into a few categories and raise tie incidence.

3

The key comparison is the scale ratio

Near complete information is a joint limit. What matters is whether reporting refinement outpaces accuracy, not whether noise becomes small in isolation.

4

The selection margin is empirically measurable

The paper links tie sensitivity to paired measurement differences, so duplicate audits, repeated readings, or matched units can reveal how fragile selection is.
